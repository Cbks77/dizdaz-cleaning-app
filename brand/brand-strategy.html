01
Brand Positioning

DizDaz is Bristol's specialist cleaning company for Airbnb hosts and short-let property operators — delivering guest-ready standards, on time, every time.

What This Means in Practice

DizDaz is not a general cleaning company that also does short-lets. DizDaz is the short-let specialist. Every message, every piece of marketing, and every client interaction should reinforce this. We are the expert choice for property managers who need consistent, reliable, guest-ready cleans between checkout and check-in.

Target Clients

Serviced Apartment Operators

Companies like YourApartment managing multiple properties in Bristol. High volume, consistent work, B2B relationship.

Airbnb Management Companies

Agencies managing multiple Airbnb listings on behalf of hosts. Consistent, high-frequency cleaning contracts.

Multi-Property Airbnb Hosts

Individual hosts running 2+ properties in Bristol who need a reliable, professional cleaning partner.

Short-Let Portfolio Managers

Property managers overseeing short-let portfolios for landlords or investors. Seeking operational reliability.

Who We're NOT Targeting

Residential weekly cleans, office cleaning, commercial deep-clean contracts, or one-off domestic bookings. Staying specialist is our strength. If a prospect doesn't fit the short-let/Airbnb profile, politely decline or refer out.

04
Brand Tone of Voice
The Principle

Friendly & Confident. Warm enough to build trust. Professional enough to win B2B contracts. Think of a highly competent local business owner who genuinely loves their work and knows their stuff — not a corporate cleaning chain, and not someone who needs to be chased.

✓ Do

  • Be warm, personal, and direct
  • Use "we" — DizDaz is a team
  • Lead with what you can do for the client
  • Reference Bristol and local knowledge
  • Acknowledge the client relationship
  • Be specific — room counts, rates, turnaround times
  • Sound like a specialist, not a generalist
  • Keep sentences short and clear

✗ Don't

  • Sound corporate, cold, or distant
  • Use cleaning-industry clichés ("sparkling clean")
  • Over-apologise or be overly deferential
  • Use jargon or technical cleaning terminology
  • Oversell — let the track record do the talking
  • Be vague about pricing or availability
  • Use ALLCAPS for emphasis
  • Use more than two exclamation marks per communication
Example — Correct Tone

"We've been working with YourApartment for five years, and every room we clean is prepared to the same guest-ready standard. If you're managing properties in Bristol and need a reliable cleaning team that knows short-let work, we'd love to talk."

Example — Wrong Tone

"AMAZING CLEANING SERVICES!!! We offer sparkling clean results at competitive prices!! Contact us TODAY for a FREE quote!!!"

Logo Usage Rules

Always

  • Use the circular badge form only
  • Maintain clear space equal to logo radius on all sides
  • Use on dark (navy) or white backgrounds
  • Apply the glowing blue box-shadow on dark backgrounds
  • Keep the gradient D letterform intact

Never

  • Stretch or squash the logo
  • Change the logo colours
  • Remove the circular crop
  • Place on mid-tone or patterned backgrounds
  • Add drop shadows other than the brand glow
  • Recreate or redraw the logo
